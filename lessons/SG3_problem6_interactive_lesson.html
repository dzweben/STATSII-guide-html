6a — Fixed effects (compute α, β, αβ)

Mini-lesson: what counts as an “effect” in a split-plot?

Even though this is a split-plot, the fixed effects are still mean differences:

  • Between main effect (damage): \(\alpha_i = \bar Y_{i\cdot\cdot} - \bar Y_{\cdot\cdot\cdot}\)
  • Within main effect (stimuli): \(\beta_k = \bar Y_{\cdot\cdot k} - \bar Y_{\cdot\cdot\cdot}\)
  • Cell interaction effect: \((\alpha\beta)_{ik} = \bar Y_{i\cdot k} - \bar Y_{i\cdot\cdot} - \bar Y_{\cdot\cdot k} + \bar Y_{\cdot\cdot\cdot}\)

Where split-plot matters: choosing the correct error term later (6b/6c).

Try it first:
  • Use the damage marginal mean for “Right.”
  • Use the stimuli marginal mean for “Mixed.”
  • Use the Right×Mixed cell mean for the interaction effect.

Given: grand mean = 7.48.

Short answer (6a):

  • αright = 7.61 − 7.48 = +0.13
  • βmixed = 7.11 − 7.48 = −0.37
  • (αβ)right,mixed = 7.17 − 7.61 − 7.11 + 7.48 = −0.07
Show solution walkthrough
Step 1 (αright): take the Right damage marginal mean and subtract the grand mean.
From SG3: Right marginal mean = 7.61.
So αright = 7.61 − 7.48 = +0.13.
Step 2 (βmixed): take the Mixed stimuli marginal mean and subtract the grand mean.
From SG3: Mixed marginal mean = 7.11.
So βmixed = 7.11 − 7.48 = −0.37.
Step 3 (αβright,mixed):
Use the cell mean (Right,Mixed) and apply the standard cell-effect identity:
Cell mean (Right,Mixed) = 7.17; Right marginal = 7.61; Mixed marginal = 7.11; Grand = 7.48.
(αβ)right,mixed = 7.17 − 7.61 − 7.11 + 7.48 = −0.07.
